PearBrowser Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 22, 2026

Overview

PearBrowser is a peer-to-peer web browser. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is used.

Data We Collect

We do not collect personal data. PearBrowser does not have analytics, telemetry, advertising, or tracking of any kind. There is no account system and no PearBrowser-operated analytics server.

Local Storage

The app stores the following data locally on your device only:

  • Bookmarks — sites you save for quick access
  • Browsing history — URLs you visit (can be cleared in Settings)
  • Settings — your preferences, relay/catalog URLs, trusted origins, and local diagnostics settings
  • Identity and grants — your local identity seed, backup/restore state, app-scoped login grants, swarm grants, and connected-app records
  • Profile fields — optional name/contact fields you choose to save and grant to apps
  • Published sites — websites you create with the built-in editor

This data stays on your device unless you choose to share, publish, sync, seed content through a relay, or grant an app access through a PearBrowser consent prompt.

Peer-to-Peer Networking

PearBrowser uses peer-to-peer (P2P) networking via the Hyperswarm protocol. When you browse P2P content:

  • Your IP address is visible to peers you connect with. This is inherent to how direct P2P networking works.
  • Content may be fetched directly from peers or via a relay gateway. A relay gateway can see the request metadata it serves, such as your IP address, requested drive key, path, and timing.
  • The Distributed Hash Table (DHT) is used for peer discovery. Your participation in the DHT is limited to finding peers for content you request.

App Permissions

Apps loaded in PearBrowser can request capabilities through the window.pear bridge. PearBrowser uses app-scoped keys and consent prompts for sensitive access such as profile fields, contacts, sign-in grants, and arbitrary Hyperswarm topics. Apps should not receive your root identity key.

Camera

PearBrowser requests camera access solely for scanning QR codes containing hyper:// addresses. No images or video are stored, transmitted, or processed beyond reading the QR code data.

Third-Party Services

PearBrowser does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. The app may connect to relay gateways that you configure or that ship as defaults to fetch P2P content over HTTPS as a fast path or fallback.

Children's Privacy

PearBrowser does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13.

Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date above.
